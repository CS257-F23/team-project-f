Voted with Majority: A justice agrees with the majority decision and supports the opinion that represents the majority's viewpoint.

Dissent: When a justice disagrees with the majority decision and expresses their opposing viewpoint in a written "dissenting opinion."

Special Concurrence: Similar to a concurrence but specific to certain aspects or points within the majority opinion, highlighting particular issues or reasoning.

Judgment of the Court: This represents the official decision of the majority of justices, constituting the majority opinion or judgment issued by the Court.

None: Some justices might abstain from expressing a specific opinion or vote, choosing not to align with the majority, dissent, or any other specific viewpoint in the case.
